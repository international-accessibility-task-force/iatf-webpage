# Code of Conduct

IATF works in public to make accessibility easier to start, easier to publish,
and easier to keep open. This community should be safe for everyone who shows
up to do that work — especially people who rely on assistive technology.

## In short

- **Be respectful.** Disagreement is fine; contempt and personal attacks are
  not.
- **Be patient.** People here have very different technical backgrounds,
  languages, and access needs.
- **Assume good intent, but say something when behavior is harmful.** A
  private nudge often works. A public correction is also fine when needed.
- **Default to plain language.** This is an accessibility project — clarity
  is part of the work.
- **Respect privacy.** Do not share private contact details, medical
  information, or identifying information about individual users of
  assistive technologies without consent.

## Unacceptable

- Harassment, intimidation, or sustained disruption in any IATF channel.
- Discriminatory comments based on disability, race, gender, sexual
  orientation, religion, age, or any other personal characteristic.
- Doxing, sharing private messages publicly, or pressuring contributors to
  reveal personal information.
- Misrepresenting accessibility claims to score points in a discussion.

## Reporting

Reports go to <contact@iatf.cc>. Reports about accessibility barriers on the
website itself go to <accessibility@iatf.cc>.

The current chair is the public point of contact for IATF and reviews
reports. See [`/governance/`](https://iatf.cc/governance/) for the chair role
and election rules.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all IATF channels and repositories: GitHub,
Discord, the website, email, and any IATF-affiliated event or call.
